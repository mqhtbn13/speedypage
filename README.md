## The Bunny CDN Thing Is Actually Useful

A lot of hosts slap "CDN included" on their marketing and mean "Cloudflare's free tier, which you could sign up for yourself in 10 minutes." SpeedyPage's CDN situation is a bit different.

They've partnered with Bunny.net — one of the faster CDN networks out there with 119+ points of presence globally. On the Premium and Enterprise plans, this comes bundled with something called the Bunny Optimizer, which automatically minifies JavaScript and CSS, compresses images, and converts them to WebP for you. On the Starter plan, CDN is available as a paid add-on.

In their own benchmark testing (using the K6.io methodology from WPHostingBenchmarks.com, simulating 1,000 concurrent users on a standard WordPress install), SpeedyPage clocked **976 requests per second**, compared to 480 for 20i, 440 for SiteGround, and 386 for GoDaddy. Take in-house benchmarks with a grain of salt, sure, but the gap is notable enough to mention.
